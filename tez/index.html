Research Questions

5 questions
RQ1

How large is IPO underpricing on BIST when measured through consecutive daily limit-up hits?

The "tavan serisi" (ceiling series) methodology captures the full pricing adjustment that standard first-day returns miss in price-limited markets.

Loughran, Ritter & Rydqvist 1994 Ljungqvist 2007
RQ2

Which firm and offer characteristics explain cross-sectional variation in underpricing?

Testing offer price, market cap, P/E, P/B ratios, sector, and market conditions as determinants of first-day returns.

Bildik & Yılmaz 2008 Kıymaz 2000
RQ3

Does herding behavior intensify in the post-IPO trading period?

CSAD methodology tests whether return dispersion declines during extreme market moves — the signature of herding.

Chang, Cheng & Khorana 2000 Economou et al. 2018
RQ4

Do SPK penalty announcements for IPO manipulation affect returns?

Event study around SPK enforcement actions tests whether regulatory penalties convey new information to the market.

Ferris & Pritchard 1994 SPK Annual Reports
RQ5

Do BIST IPOs exhibit long-run overreaction, and can a contrarian strategy exploit it?

Testing the overreaction hypothesis: do the highest-underpriced IPOs subsequently underperform, enabling a contrarian long-short strategy?

De Bondt & Thaler 1985 Loughran & Ritter 1995

Key Findings

6 findings
📈

Extreme Underpricing

Mean 67.8%, median 33.1%. Average limit-up streak: 4.4 days. 67.2% of IPOs hit the daily limit on day one.

🔬

P/B Only Significant Factor

Cross-sectional OLS (HC3 robust SE) shows P/B ratio is the sole significant determinant (p=0.040). R² = 10–21%.

🐑

No Herding Detected

CSAD γ₂ = −0.079 (p=0.706). No statistically significant herding during 2020–2025 with 59% test power.

⚖️

SPK Penalties: −7.4% CAAR

47 penalty events show cumulative −7.4% abnormal returns over 30 days post-announcement. Significant negative drift.

📉

Short-term Overreaction

Post-tavan 5-day return: −3.3% (significant). Initial euphoria reverses quickly once limit-up streak ends.

🔄

Long-run Recovery

365-day post-tavan return: +72.6% (significant). Contrarian long-short exploits initial mispricing over 1 year.

Methodology

4 methods
M1

Limit-Up Underpricing

Consecutive daily limit-up methodology following Loughran, Ritter & Rydqvist (1994). Returns measured from first unconstrained close.

M2

Event Study

Market-model abnormal returns with Boehmer (1991) test. Per-event σ estimation around SPK penalty announcements.

M3

CSAD Herding Test

Cross-Sectional Absolute Deviation following Chang, Cheng & Khorana (2000). Tests for non-linear return dispersion.

M4

Cross-Sectional OLS

HC3 heteroscedasticity-robust standard errors. 4 model specifications with firm and offer characteristics.
